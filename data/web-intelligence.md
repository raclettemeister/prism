## 🇪🇺 EU Digital Sovereignty: Latest Update (April 2026)

### What Changed

In April 2026, the European Commission awarded its **Sovereign Cloud call for tender**, enabling EU institutions, bodies, offices, and agencies to procure sovereign cloud services for **up to €180 million over 6 years**.

The tender was launched in October 2025, as part of the Commission's efforts to strengthen the digital sovereignty posture of EU entities.

The Commission awarded **four contracts** to ensure diversification and resilience, explicitly to avoid lock-in by a single provider. Winners include a Belgian-French-Luxembourgish partnership led by **Proximus**, using services from **S3NS** (a Thales–Google Cloud joint venture), **Clarence**, and **Mistral**.

To assess providers, the Commission developed a **Cloud Sovereignty Framework** with eight measurable objectives and introduced **Sovereignty Effectiveness Assurance Levels (SEAL)**, ranging from SEAL-0 (no sovereignty) to SEAL-4 (full EU supply chain). Providers needed to reach at least SEAL-2 to be eligible.

---

### Why It Matters

Before the Sovereign Cloud Framework was developed, it was not possible to measure digital sovereignty, making it difficult to introduce it as a requirement in procurement. The Framework now provides a **standardised, concrete method** to assess cloud services, moving away from abstract principles.

The Commission states this sets a **benchmark for secure, compliant, and values-based cloud adoption** across the EU public sector, and demonstrates that European providers are closing the technology gap with US hyperscalers.

This decision fits into a broader EU push: on November 18, 2025, France and Germany led a **European Digital Sovereignty Summit** in Berlin, aligning on AI, data governance, and public digital infrastructure. On December 5, 2025, EU member states formalized the **"European Digital Sovereignty Declaration"** — a non-binding but strategically significant document.
