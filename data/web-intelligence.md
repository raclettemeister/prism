### 🔑 What Changed

**1. Unemployment Benefits Capped (Effective 1 March 2026 — flagship reform)**

Effective 1 March 2026, Belgium radically revised the conditions of access to unemployment and integration benefits. Entitlement to unemployment benefits is now limited to a maximum of **24 months** (12 months as standard, plus up to 12 additional months depending on professional experience). This is a major change: previously, benefits were available for an **unlimited duration**.

From 1 March 2026, fully unemployed Belgian residents who meet the eligibility conditions are entitled to Belgian unemployment benefits for 12 months, with additional months depending on work experience.

Belgium became the **last EU country** to abolish unlimited unemployment benefits. The change was presented as a way to encourage faster return to work and align Belgium with its EU peers.

**Transitional waves already underway:**
The first wave saw benefit entitlements ending on **1 January 2026** (those in the third benefit period unemployed for 20+ years). The second wave ends on **1 March 2026** (those in the third period unemployed for 8–20 years).

---

**2. New Access Rules for New Unemployment Claimants (from 1 March 2026)**

Anyone who loses their job and submits a benefit application from **March 1, 2026**, will be subject to the new 24-month rule (12-month base period + 12 additional months depending on professional history).

---

**3. Integration Benefits Also Capped**

Entitlement to integration benefits (previously a safety-net for young job-seekers) is now limited to a maximum of **one year**.

---

**4. Wage Indexation Reform (January 2026)**

A hybrid wage indexation model applies in 2026: **full indexation only applies to the first €4,000 of gross monthly salary**. The portion above that threshold will not be indexed.

---

**5. Night Work & Labour Flexibility ("Arizona Reform")**

From **April 1, 2026**, the Arizona reform facilitates night work by shifting its start from 8:00 PM to 11:00 PM for e-commerce and distribution sectors. Hours between 8:00 PM and 11:00 PM are now considered "evening work."

---

**6. Work Incapacity / Medical Force Majeure**

From **January 1, 2026**, the uninterrupted incapacity period required to trigger the medical force majeure termination procedure was reduced from 9 months to **6 months**.
