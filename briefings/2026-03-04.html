🌍 WORLD LENS

Iran, oil, and the European cost floor. China is moving to lean on Russian oil as the Iran crisis chokes supply — a geopolitical displacement that ripples through energy markets in ways that are directly material to a Brussels food retailer. The mechanism is indirect but reliable: when Middle East supply tightens, European refiners face pressure on feedstock costs; when Chinese demand shifts toward Russian crude, it reconfigures global oil price dynamics. Belgium imports virtually all its energy. A sustained Iran disruption does not produce a single dramatic price spike — it produces a slow, grinding elevation of the cost floor for heating, refrigeration, and logistics that squeezes thin-margin food retail over quarters, not weeks.

For Chez Julien specifically: the more immediate concern is that consumer confidence in Brussels tends to track energy price anxiety. A household watching its heating bill climb spends less on specialty food. This is not a crisis scenario — it is a slow-squeeze backdrop that makes Operation Autonomy's efficiency goals more urgent, not less.

Belgium VAT: still in flux, still material. Shortly before the end of 2025, the Belgian federal government reached an agreement introducing significant changes to several reduced VAT rates, primarily affecting hospitality, catering, leisure, and food sectors, with all VAT changes initially applying from 1 March 2026. However, as of 13 February 2026, Belgium's government delayed the planned 1 March 2026 reduced VAT rate rises — including from 6% to 12% on takeaway food — following a strongly critical advisory opinion from the Council of State.

The delay does not mean the changes are dead — it means the timeline is uncertain. The structural pressure remains: Belgian fiscal consolidation will eventually extract revenue from the hospitality and food sector. For a specialty food shop owner, this is a planning variable that needs a confirmed date before it becomes an operational decision. Monitor official communications. Do not assume the delay is a reversal.
