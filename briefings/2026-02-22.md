## üèóÔ∏è BUILD WATCH

**The harness wars are consolidating around two poles.** This week's evidence points to a fork in the market: heavyweight orchestration frameworks (OpenAI Codex harness, ACP protocol by Zed/JetBrains) versus lightweight skill-file systems (`obra/superpowers`, Claude Pilot from Feb 17 briefing). The lightweight pole is winning on adoption speed. `superpowers` installs in one command; the Codex harness requires reading a 4,000-word engineering post. For solo non-technical founders, the lightweight pole is the only viable one in the near term.

**"Why is Claude an Electron app?"** is a more important question than it looks. The `dbreunig.com` piece surfaces a structural insight: coding agents are excellent at the first 90% of development but the last 10% ‚Äî edge cases, real-world messiness, ongoing support ‚Äî remains hard and human-dependent. For a founder building micro-software: this is not a reason to stop. It's a reason to scope micro. Build things where the last 10% is small, not large. Chez Julien BI, Now Page Bot, and The Cockpit are all well-scoped. The Second Game is the first project where scope discipline will matter.

**Local/edge AI inference is becoming a genuine product category.** `zclaw` (AI personal assistant in 888KB on an ESP32) and the Juno Labs piece ("every company building your AI assistant is now an ad company") point to the same structural trend: privacy-motivated users will pay for local inference hardware. This is not directly actionable for Julien today, but it's a trajectory worth tracking. The business model question ‚Äî sell hardware + software, not data ‚Äî is a template that applies to micro-SaaS thinking more broadly.
