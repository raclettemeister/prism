🧱 BUILDER INTELLIGENCE

Methodology beat:

Simon Willison published a sharp anti-pattern: don't file pull requests with code you haven't reviewed yourself. If you open a PR with hundreds of lines of agent-generated code you haven't verified, you're delegating the actual work to others. For you as a non-technical CEO supervising AI-generated code: you are the reviewer. If you're shipping Cursor output you haven't read, this is your blind spot.

The Harness Engineering debate from Latent Space adds a useful frame: Noam Brown of OpenAI argues that before reasoning models, engineers built complex agentic scaffolds — but reasoning models have replaced the need for most of that scaffolding, and the remaining scaffolds will also be replaced as models improve. Translation for you: don't over-engineer your prompting systems. The model is getting smarter faster than your scaffolding can.
