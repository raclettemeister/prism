üß± BUILDER INTELLIGENCE

Methodology beat:

Addy Osmani published a sharp piece arguing that "something shifted recently with agentic engineering" ‚Äî not a marginal improvement, but a step change where "the center of gravity of the craft moved." The key insight for a non-technical builder: at the scale of many parallel agents, "the difference between mediocre output and exceptional output comes down almost entirely to the quality of your specification." Vague thinking doesn't just slow you down ‚Äî it multiplies across every agent run. This maps directly onto your known cockpit problem (things getting implemented without your control): the fix isn't a better AI, it's a tighter spec before you press go.

Simon Willison's Agentic Engineering Patterns series now has four chapters. Chapter 3, "First run the tests," advises starting every new agent session with a four-word prompt that simultaneously forces the agent to discover the test suite, learn how to run it, and establish a baseline before any changes are made. Willison's argument is that automated tests "are no longer optional when working with coding agents," since they are vital for ensuring AI-generated code actually does what it claims to do.

From latent.space's AINews: Andrej Karpathy argues that coding agents crossed a qualitative threshold since December ‚Äî from brittle demos to sustained, long-horizon task completion. He gives an example of delegating an end-to-end local deployment with minimal intervention.

Micro-SaaS Radar:
